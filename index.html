About Assam University

Founded in 1994, Assam University Silchar is a Central University that stands at the crossroads of knowledge, culture, and resilience. Nestled in the serene hills of Southern Assam, it reflects the spirit of the Northeast ‚Äî deeply rooted, richly diverse, and constantly evolving.

The university encompasses 16 schools and 35+ departments, offering programs across disciplines such as humanities, sciences, technology, law, management, and environmental studies. Its verdant campus, spread across over 600 acres, is more than a learning space ‚Äî it is a living ecosystem where ideas grow, disciplines merge, and traditions blend with innovation.

What truly distinguishes Assam University is its commitment to inclusivity and regional empowerment. The institution plays a vital role in preserving indigenous knowledge systems, promoting linguistic diversity, and addressing the socio-economic challenges of the Barak Valley and the wider Northeast.

Home to thousands of students from across India, AUS fosters a multicultural environment where debates are encouraged, creativity is celebrated, and the spirit of inquiry thrives. The university has contributed to policy, grassroots change, and research that directly impacts lives in remote and underserved areas.

Dr. R. Ravi Kannan is an Indian surgical oncologist renowned for his transformative work in making cancer care accessible to underserved communities. After leading the surgical oncology department at Adyar Cancer Institute in Chennai, he moved to Silchar, Assam, in 2007 to head the Cachar Cancer Hospital and Research Centre (CCHRC). IB Ubhadia

General Manager, Rosekandy Tea Estate | Former Chairman, Surma Valley Branch, ITA Senior Tea Industry Executive | Plantation Management Expert | Advocate for Tea Labor Welfare

Ishvarlal Bhudarbhai Ubhadia is the General Manager of Rosekandy Tea Estate in Cachar, Assam, bringing over 35 years of experience in the tea industry. He has also served as Chairman of the Surma Valley Branch of the Indian Tea Association, where he addressed challenges like labor absenteeism affecting tea productivity in South Assam. Under his leadership, Rosekandy Tea Estate, established in 1860 and spanning 560 hectares, produces approximately 14 lakh kg of tea annually. Ubhadia's tenure has seen both advancements and challenges, including labor strikes and administrative inquiries, reflecting the complexities of managing one of the region's prominent tea estates.(Smasilchar, The Assam Tribune, Telegraph India)

Dr. Vijender Singh Chauhan, also known by his pen name Masijeevi, is an Indian educator, motivational speaker, and social media influencer. Born on July 8 in Bhajanpura, Delhi, he pursued higher education at Delhi University, earning degrees in Arts, Education, Hindi, Philosophy, and a Ph.D. in Media and Literature. Since 2005, he has been teaching Hindi at Zakir Husain Delhi College, Delhi University. Dr. Chauhan is renowned for mentoring UPSC aspirants through mock interviews at Drishti IAS and is a sought-after speaker at events like TEDx and JoshTalks.
